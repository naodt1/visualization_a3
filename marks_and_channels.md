### Marks and Channels

#### Parallel Coordinates
Marks
1. Lines, each line represents a data point.

CHannels
1. Position: The intersection of the line on the two parallel axes encodes the values. Each axis represent a quantitative attribute.
2. Color Hue: Encodes the range of temperature. Red to Blue shows high temp to low temp values. This is only there to help follow a polyline and see the intersections more clearly.

#### Parallel Sets
Marks
1. Horizontal bars, each bar represents a category.
2. Ribbons

Channels
1. Length of bars: The percentage of data that occurs in the specific category.
2. Vertical position of the bar: Encodes which attribute the bar represents.
3. Horizontal position of the bar: Encodes which category of a certain attribute the bar represents.
4. Width of the ribbon: Encodes the size of the set.
5. Color: Encodes the categories of the primary axis (Air Pressure) to trach the path easily.

#### Your Chosen Time Visualization
Marks
1. Lines to represent the change in attribute.

Channels
For the temperature plot:
1. Horizontal postion: Encodes the year.
2. Vertical position: Encodes the absolute minimum temperature value for the year in Celcius.
3. Color hue: The station where the termperature was recroded.

For the humidity plot:
1. Horizontal postion: Encodes the year.
2. Vertical position: Encodes the mean humidity level for a season in the year.
3. Color hue: Encodes the seasons.
