### Answers to the questions

#### 1. In which year and in which weather station has the lowest temperature been recorded?
Based on the chart, the minimum teperature recorded from 2011 to 2020 was -24.5°C and it was recorded in Carisfeld in January 2012.

#### 2. Are there seasonal changes in humidity?
From our visualization, we can see that winter (from December to February) is generally the most humid season with humidity levels above 85%. And summer (from June to August) is the least humid season. On average, autumn (from September to November) is closer to winter in humidity levels while spring (from March to May) is closer to summer.

#### 3. Is there some kind of relation between temperature and humidity?
The parallel coordinates chart shows that higher temperatures (greater than 20°C) usually have lower humidity levels (less than 75%). The we see a clear intersection between the two plots, high temp and low humidity vs low temp and high humidity. We can say that there is an inverse relation. For the mid-range values, there is no obvious pattern.

#### 4. a) Is air pressure lower by the sea or at continental locations? 
According to Meteorological Scale Reference, low air pressure is considered to be less than 980 hPa. Additionally, we researched what are can be defined as coastal land and UN suggestes that less than 100km distance from sea is considered coastal. 
Therefore, our parallel sets chart shows that low air pressure is largely found in continental locations. Except for some very minor outliers, aprroximately 100% of the low air pressure readings are from continental lands.

#### 4. b) Is there a difference in air pressure between sea-level stations and mountain-based stations? 
According to Körner et al. (2011) locations less than 100m above sea are considered sea-level locations. The rest are considered ad mountaineous regions.

Our parallel sets show the sea-level or lowland stations recorded air pressure values from medium to high (> 980hPa) while mountaineous stations recorded low air pressure. We cna say that at greater distances from sea-level, the air pressure lowers.
